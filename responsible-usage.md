# Using Tools Responsibly

## Why It's Important to Use Free Tools Properly

Many of these resources are provided free to students to support learning and personal development. Using them responsibly ensures:

- **Continued availability** for future students
- **Compliance with terms of service** to avoid account suspension
- **Ethical use** of educational benefits
- **Support for companies** that invest in education

## Commercial Use Restrictions

### Do NOT Use for Commercial Purposes

Many student tools explicitly prohibit commercial use. **Do not use these tools for:**

- Running a business or startup (unless the tool explicitly allows it)
- Providing paid services to clients
- Commercial product development intended for sale
- Any revenue-generating activities

### Acceptable Uses

These tools are generally acceptable for:

- Personal learning projects
- Academic assignments and coursework
- Portfolio projects (non-commercial)
- Hackathon projects
- Research and experimentation
- Open-source contributions

## What Can You Use for Business?

If you're working on a commercial project or business, consider these alternatives:

### Open Source Tools (Free for Commercial Use)

| Category | Open Source Alternatives |
|----------|-------------------------|
| **Cloud Hosting** | Self-hosted solutions, DigitalOcean, Linode |
| **Databases** | PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB Community |
| **Development** | VS Code, Eclipse, NetBeans |
| **Version Control** | Git (self-hosted), GitLab Community Edition |
| **Project Management** | OpenProject, Taiga, Redmine |
| **Analytics** | Matomo, Plausible (self-hosted) |

### Free Tiers (Commercial-Friendly)

Many services offer free tiers that allow commercial use:

- **Vercel** - Free tier for hobby projects
- **Netlify** - Free tier with commercial use allowed
- **Supabase** - Free tier for small projects
- **PlanetScale** - Free tier for databases
- **Railway** - Free tier with usage limits

## Best Practices

1. **Read the Terms of Service** - Always check if commercial use is allowed
2. **Upgrade When Needed** - Transition to paid plans when your project becomes commercial
3. **Be Transparent** - Don't try to hide commercial use under student accounts
4. **Support the Ecosystem** - Consider paying for tools when you can afford it



If you're unsure whether your use case is appropriate, check the specific tool's terms of service or contact their support team.
